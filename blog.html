Top 5 Roofing Trends in 2026

Explore the latest innovations in roofing materials and designs that are transforming the construction industry. From green roofs to solar tiles, discover what's new. Sustainable roofing is becoming a priority for both residential and commercial projects. Green roofs not only provide insulation but also reduce stormwater runoff. Solar tiles integrate seamlessly with traditional roofing, generating electricity without compromising aesthetics. Cool roofs reflect more sunlight and absorb less heat, lowering energy bills. Metal roofing is gaining popularity for its durability and recyclability. Finally, smart roofs with integrated sensors can detect leaks and monitor structural health.

How to Prepare a BOQ Like a Pro

Learn the essential steps to create an accurate Bill of Quantities that keeps your construction project on budget. A well-prepared BOQ is crucial for tendering and cost control. Start with a thorough site visit and detailed drawings. List all materials with precise specifications. Use standard measurement methods to ensure consistency. Include allowances for waste and contingencies. Review and cross-check with suppliers for current prices. Finally, structure the BOQ in a clear, logical format that contractors can easily understand. Regular updates during the project help track variations.

Rapid urbanisation presents unique challenges. Discover how we're addressing infrastructure, housing, and sustainability in Juba and beyond. Renovation Tips for Old Buildings

Breathing new life into older structures requires careful planning. From structural assessments to modern finishes, we share our renovation expertise. Always start with a thorough survey to identify hidden issues like termites or outdated wiring. Preserve original features where possible—they add character. Upgrade insulation and windows for energy efficiency. Choose materials that complement the building's era but offer modern performance. Plan the work in phases to minimise disruption. Finally, obtain necessary permits, especially for heritage buildings.
